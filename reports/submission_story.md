Hook: Global variety, not fame, is Twitch’s loyalty cheat code.

Context: Twitch Streamers Live Data 2025 captures over three million viewer-channel interactions, revealing who actually sticks around. Most community conversations assume smaller creators build deeper bonds, yet we rarely quantify it. This project reframes loyalty by counting repeat interactions per viewer and mapping how many audience clusters each creator truly touches.

Method: Cleaned and validated the raw CSV via `pipeline.py`, then used pandas to compute loyalty ratios, diversity bands, and superfan concentration. Visualized the findings in a reproducible notebook plus four publication-ready charts exported to `figures/`.

Key Finding: Once a channel reaches 100+ language or geo clusters, median loyalty jumps 45% compared to creators stuck in ≤3 clusters. Mega channels don’t lose loyalty—they amplify it, especially when their communities are diversified. Secondary visuals show mid-tier creators rely on ten superfans for half their engagement while viewers graze across 291 channels.

Why it matters: Creators chasing scale alone risk plateaus unless they intentionally program for new clusters and reduce superfan dependency. Platforms and sponsors can spot resilient partners by monitoring diversity bands alongside loyalty ratios.

Takeaway: Engineer loyalty: expand into new clusters, measure superfan reliance, and design every stream for viewers who always have 300 other tabs open.
